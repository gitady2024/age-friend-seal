Global Demographic Transition

The convergence of two macroeconomic forces is reshaping the active workforce and the consumer market across the globe.

📉 Global Demographic Winter

The global fertility rate has dropped from 3.3 births per woman in 1990 to just 2.3 today. With the replacement level at 2.1, nearly two-thirds of the world's population now lives in countries with declining rates. In East Asia (South Korea at 0.72) and Latin America (1.8), the future shortage of active labor puts severe fiscal pressure on pension and healthcare systems.

👵 Senior Preponderance & Longevity

Major healthcare breakthroughs are drastically increasing active lifespans, making the over-50 segment the fastest-growing. This generation accounts for more than 50% of global spending. The Silver Economy is transitioning from a fiscal risk into a mandatory engine for innovation, adapted flexible employment, and specialized consumption.

Data supported and studied by leading global organizations such as the World Bank, IDB Lab, WHO, and ECLAC.

The 5 Global Foundations of Certification

Our framework integrates the most demanding global standards to guide companies in adopting pro-age policies with high social and economic impact.

Based on ISO 25550:2022, ILO and UN Treaties

Defines Sello guidelines to eradicate corporate ageism, ensure retention and employability of senior professionals, and leverage multigenerational teams as a driver for innovation.

  • Retention & Career Longevity: Programs to conserve senior intellectual capital.
  • Reverse Mentoring: Bidirectional training between junior and senior staff.
  • Blind Resumes: Removal of birth dates and photos during candidate screenings.
View reference standard ISO 25550 ↗

Based on ISO 25551:2021, WHO and PAHO

Standardizes corporate support for "sandwich generation" employees, preventing premature career exit by harmonizing work with eldercare duties for dependent relatives.

  • Schedule Adaptability: Direct channels for eldercare medical appointments.
  • Burnout Mitigation: Dedicated emotional support for caregivers.
  • Eldercare Leave: Flexible time banks designated for eldercare emergencies.
View reference standard ISO 25551 ↗

Based on ISO 25556:2025, WHO (Age-Friendly) and IDB Lab

Regulates channel and tech accessibility to ensure that massive services digitalization does not exclude the over-50 population, capturing the huge potential of the Silver Economy.

  • AgeTech Touch Design: Interfaces compensating for visual contrast reduction.
  • AI & Bias Auditing: Reviewing automated algorithms to prevent age discrimination.
  • Direct Human Escape: Direct one-tap buttons to talk to real support agents.
View reference standard ISO 25556 ↗

World Health Organization

Standards for adapting physical environments, accessibility of analog channels coexisting with digital ones, and screening intrinsic capacity.

  • Physical environments and buildings free of access barriers.
  • Alternative analog/telephone communication channels.
  • Community-clinical screening of functional capacities.
View WHO programs ↗

International Labour Organization

Global political-legal treaty on labor matters. Protects equal opportunities, prohibits ageist dismissal, and promotes flexible retirement schemes.

  • Equal opportunities without chronological discrimination.
  • Corporate transition and gradual retirement programs.
  • Labor protection in corporate restructurings.
